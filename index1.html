About me

I am PhD student of Economics at the Stony Brook University (SBU), where I also have taught, as instructor, undergraduate level courses such as Introduction to Economics and Public Finance.

My research interests are focused within fields such as Industrial Organization, Game Theory and Applied Microeconomics. Particularly I am interested in using Game Theory on networks to explain, predict and model the strategic behavior of firms that operate and compete in network industries, such as telecommunications and transportation.

I received a MA degree in Applied Economics from the University of Oklahoma in 2012, funded by the Fulbright Fellowship Program. In 2005, I received a MA degree in International Trade and Economic Cooperation from the Kyung Hee University (South Korea), funded by the Korean International Cooperation Agency (KOICA).

From 2005 to 2006 I worked as a Research Assistant of the Instituto de Estudios Peruanos (IEP) and as an Economics Adviser of the Secretariat of Communications of the Ministry of Transportation and Communications of Peru. Then, from 2006 to 2010 I worked in the Department of Economic Studies of the Antitrust Agency of Peru (Instituto Nacional de Defensa de la Competencia y Protection de la Propiedad Intelectual - INDECOPI).
